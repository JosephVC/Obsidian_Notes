- - Proof-of-Stake (PoS) is a consensus mechanism that reduces the need for computational power by using randomly selected validators to confirm transactions and create new blocks, significantly lowering energy consumption compared to Proof-of-Work (PoW).
	- you can be become a validator only if you already have coins to stake
		- Etherium requires you have at least 32 ETH to operate a "node"
			- Node is just a participant in a blockchain that validates transactions and creates new blocks based on what is staked 
	- if you already need coins to become a validator, where are those coins supposed to come from?

- Validators in a PoS system must stake a certain amount of cryptocurrency as collateral to be selected for block validation, promoting network security and reducing environmental impact.
	- validators monitor activity on the blockchain and record transactions

- Ethereum's switch from PoW to PoS reduced its energy usage by 99.84%, highlighting the potential of PoS to address sustainability concerns in the cryptocurrency industry.

- A potential downside of PoS is the high entry cost for becoming a validator, as seen with Ethereum's requirement of 32 ETH, which may lead to network centralization.

- The PoS mechanism also mitigates risks of a 51% attack by making it prohibitively expensive to control the majority of staked cryptocurrency, thus maintaining network integrity.

|Proof of Stake|Proof of Work|
|---|---|
|Block creators are called validators|Block creators are called miners|
|Participants must own coins or tokens to become validators|Participants must buy equipment and energy to become a miner|
|Energy efficient|Not energy efficient|
|Security through community control|Robust security due to expensive upfront requirement|
|Validators receive transaction fees as rewards|Miners receive block rewards and fees|

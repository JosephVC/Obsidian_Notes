- opened by an individual and not through an employer
	- as such does not benefit from employer matching
 
- You can contribute to the Roth IRA after you change jobs as it's not tied to any employer
 
- you can choose what financial institution holds the IRA
 
- **Income Limit:*** individuals paid over $161k/year are not eligible for Roth IRA contributions
	- a Roth 401k has no income limit

- there is a **required minimum distribution (RMD)*** a person must make at a certain age (age 75 for those born after 1960)
	- if you fail to meet the RMD requirement there might be penalties of up to 25% of the shortfall - this can drop to 10% if you correct this
	- you can defer the RMD if you are still employed and NOT a 5% owner of your company at the given age limit

- ***Withdrawal of funds***
	- you can withdraw any funds up to the contributions made without penalty or taxes
		- the ***earnings*** have a 10% penalty for if you withdraw before 59.5
		- for certain major life events, you can withdraw funds without penalty or taxes depending on whether you've had the fund for more or less than 5 years
	- per the SECURE 2.0 ACT people can access up to $1k of retirement savings for emergency personal for family use without the usual 10% withdrawal penalty

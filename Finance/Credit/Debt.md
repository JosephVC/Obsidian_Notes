**Secured Debt**
- borrowers put up some asset as collateral for a loan
- in the event of a default the lender can seize the given assets to collect the advanced funds
- common types
	- mortgages
		- needs a credit score of at least 620
	- car loans
- home equity loan - backed by the borrower's home
- as you have collateral, you can get a lower interest rate
- secured loans can be easier to get even with shitty credit
- secured loans can come with longer payment terms
- overall prevents abusive behavior by debt collectors


**Unsecured Debt**
- credit cards, medical bills, personal loans, etc.
	- there are some secured credit cards, which are backed by an initial deposit
- any  debt not tied to an asset, such as homes
	- creditors have no claim on your property
- personal loans
- private student loans
- medical debt
- apartment leases
- cellphone and utility bills
- auto repossession overall balances
- short selling real estate
	- if you sell a property for $10k under what it was worth you will settle debts for what  you sold the properly for but you will continue making payments on that remaining $10k

**Priority Vs Non-Priority**
- priority 
	- taxes, alimony, child support, criminal fines
	- cannot get discharged through bankruptcy and are paid first
- non-priority
	- credit-card balances
	- personal loans
	- utilities
	- medical bills
	- student loans
	- paid for by whatever money is left after paying priority debts 
		- if nothing left, then non-priority debts are discharged and go unpain

**Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA) Rules** 
- sets limits on how 3rd party debt collectors 
- collectors must provide a written validation notice within 5 days of initial contact, including details about the debt and options for dispute
- prohibits debt collectors
- it's best to send a debt collector a letter via certified mail to get tracking and return receipt in order for getting then to stop calling you

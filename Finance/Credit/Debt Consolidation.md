**Basics of How it Works**
- the given debt consolidation company will negotiate with a creditor on your behalf to get them to accept less than the amount you owe
	- a creditor might view this as better than getting nothing at all
- you pay in to what amounts to a savings account managed by the debt relief company - one that you can access online anytime
- if a deal is struck, the creditor will be paid from the savings account you created
- the potential savings on dept popularized by the debt relief company 
- there is a settlement fee that will be a certain percentage of the total deb you owe
	- the settlement fee cannot collect the settlement fee until it successfully settles a debt


**Debt Consolidation Loan**
- debt consolidation loans might be safer as you still pay creditors rather than default 
- one big loan to take  care of all the smaller loans
	- the idea is that the big loan will have a smaller interest rate than the credit card, etc. loans


**Risks**
- Damage to credit
- interest and fees continue to accumulate
- Debt collectors and creditors will still bother you
- Debt that is forgiven may be considered taxable


**Alternatives**
- Call creditors yourself and negotiate
- consolidation loan
- other companies run a **debt management plan**
- Bankruptcy

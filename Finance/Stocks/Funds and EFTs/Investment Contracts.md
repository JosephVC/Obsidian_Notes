**GIC**
- Guaranteed Investment Contract

- offer fixed interest rate and principle return

- - The security of GICs depends on the financial stability of the issuing insurance company, as they are not federally insured.

- Low-interest rates on GICs may not keep pace with inflation, potentially reducing purchasing power over time.

- Synthetic GICs include diversified portfolios protected from interest rate volatility, unlike traditional GICs.

- GICs are commonly used in 401(k) plans as part of a stable value fund, offering a low-risk investment option.

- like a certificate of deposit, but purchased by large institutions

- these are not FDIC insured

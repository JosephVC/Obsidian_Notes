- funded with after tax dollars rather than pretax, so there is no tax deduction for the 401k contribution
	- income tax is paid on the money before it is deposited in the account, so there is no income tax payment made when funds are withdrawn 
	- ***employers can match your contributions with pretax dollars, so these matching funds are placed in a traditional 401k***
		- if you start taking distributions on your Roth 401k funds you might need to pay taxes on what comes from the employer matching

- contributions are made directly from one's paycheck with potential employer matching

- offered through employer and may have matching contributions

- might have higher contribution limits 

- penalty-free withdrawals of contributions

- access to Roth 401k funds before age 59.5 is limited
	- 10% penalty on 

- do not require minimum distributions

- lack income limits for contributions but limit investment choices defined by employer

- there is no required recommended minimum distribution 

- for 2025, the max an employee can contribute is $23,500

- **catch up contributions*** can be made in a given year if you turn 50 by the end of the year
	- these are adjusted for inflation and subject to a cost of living adjustment

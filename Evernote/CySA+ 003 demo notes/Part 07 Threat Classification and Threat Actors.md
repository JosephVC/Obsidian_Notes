**Unknown Threats**

* signature-based tools don't work very well, as there is no set signature
	* look for behaviors rather than signature
* very advanced
* very new: 0-day

![[./_resources/Part_07_Threat_Classification_and_Threat_Actors.resources/image.png]]

**Bug Bounties**

* reward for finding vulnerabilities

**Bad Actors**

* can learn the behaviors of bad actors
* **APT (Advanced Persistent Threat)**
	* Advanced tools, coordinated group
		* may start with commodity malware then pull out custom malware
	* Persistent, often undetected
	* Threat, malicious actors
	* <https://www.mandiant.com/resources/insights/apt-groups>
	* **Exam assumes that APTs are well funded**
		* may or may not be well-funded, but still need to be skilled and persistent
* **Organized Crime**
	* Cyberterrorism
* hacktivists
* nation states
* script kiddies
* recreational hackers
* pro hackers
* suicide hackers
	* don't care if they get caught
* insider threat
	* intentional or unintentional
	* dangerous as access is already granted
	* anger, financial gain, bribes, threats
	* shadow IT
		* devices and services that are not approved by company

**Commodity Malware**

* malware for sale
* <https://github.com/EmpireProject/Empire>
	* uses templates, so can be detected
* darkweb
